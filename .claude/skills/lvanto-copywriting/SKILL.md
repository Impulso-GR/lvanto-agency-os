# Lvanto Copywriting Skill

## 1. Purpose

Produce clear, conversion-oriented copy for Lvanto client projects. Every output must move the reader toward a specific action.

---

## 2. When to Use

- Writing or reviewing landing page sections
- Creating ad copy for Meta or Google
- Drafting social media posts or captions
- Writing WhatsApp messages or sequences
- Structuring proposal copy or offer descriptions
- Generating hook variations or CTA options

---

## 3. Inputs to Request

Before writing, ask for:

- **Product or service**: what is being sold or communicated?
- **Target audience**: who is this for? (role, pain, context)
- **Goal of the copy**: click, reply, form fill, sale, awareness?
- **Tone**: formal, conversational, urgent, aspirational?
- **Platform**: landing page, Instagram, WhatsApp, email, proposal?
- **Language**: Spanish (default) or other?
- **Any existing copy or references**: to match or improve?

---

## 4. Copywriting Framework

Apply this sequence when structuring any copy piece:

1. **Target audience** — name who you are speaking to. Be specific.
2. **Pain / problem** — state the problem they already feel. Do not invent pain.
3. **Desired outcome** — describe the result they want after solving the problem.
4. **Offer** — present the solution clearly. What is it, what does it do, what does it include?
5. **Proof / trust** — add a result, testimonial, credential, or guarantee.
6. **Objection handling** — address the most likely reason they would not act.
7. **CTA** — one clear, specific action. No ambiguity.

Not every format needs all seven steps. Use only what fits the piece.

---

## 5. Output Formats

### Landing page copy
- Headline + subheadline
- Benefit bullets (3–5)
- CTA button text
- Optional: objection block or trust line

### Ad copy
- Hook (first line stops the scroll)
- Body (problem → solution → proof)
- CTA (one action)
- Headline variation (for A/B)

### Social post
- Hook line
- Body (2–4 short paragraphs or lines)
- CTA or engagement question
- Hashtags only if requested

### WhatsApp message
- Short, conversational, one idea per message
- Avoid walls of text
- CTA at the end, low friction (e.g., "¿Te interesa que te cuente más?")

### Proposal section
- Offer title
- What the client gets (deliverables)
- Why it solves their specific problem
- Investment or next step

### Hook ideas
- Deliver 3–5 variations
- Formats: question, bold statement, contrast, result, curiosity gap
- Label each type

---

## 6. Quality Rules

- **Benefits over features**: say what it does for the client, not what it is.
- **Clear CTA**: one action per piece. Never two.
- **No generic marketing language**: avoid "soluciones innovadoras", "de calidad", "líder en el mercado", and similar filler.
- **Adapt tone to client brand**: match the client's voice — do not impose a default style.
- **Write in Spanish by default** unless the operator requests another language.
- **Short sentences**: if a sentence has more than two clauses, split it.
- **Read it aloud test**: if it sounds unnatural when read aloud, rewrite it.
