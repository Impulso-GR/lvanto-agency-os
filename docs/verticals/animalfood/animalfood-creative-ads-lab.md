# AnimalFood — Creative Ads Lab

> AnimalFood-only. Read first: `animalfood-context.md`, `animalfood-content-system.md`, `animalfood-paid-traffic-system.md`. Keep separate from Lvanto, Sileoni, and future clients.
> Template: `templates/campaigns/animalfood-creative-ads-sheet-template.md`.

## 1. Purpose
An operating system for planning **Meta Ads creatives and organic-to-paid experiments** for AnimalFood — based on research, hypotheses, creative quality, brand consistency, and measurable outcomes. It helps Gonzalo decide: what angles to test, which organic content should become an ad, what assets to prepare, what Aranza can adapt, what Gonzalo must design personally, which metric decides success, and when to pause, iterate, or scale.

## 2. Research-First Requirement
Before proposing any creative or test, check evidence: recent Instagram/Meta data, account insights, previous content results, competitor observation, recent platform trends, Meta Ads results, market data, public sources, or user-provided data. If data is unavailable, **label the recommendation as an assumption and request the critical missing data**. Do not present outdated or generic ideas as fact. For every major recommendation include: **evidence used · confidence (High/Medium/Low) · risk · metric to validate · next action.** Ask Gonzalo before using browser/MCP/API/connected tools.

## 3. Creative Strategy Principles
- Good taste **+** strategy **+** performance data.
- **Mobile-first.**
- **9:16 first**, 4:5 secondary.
- **One clear benefit** per piece.
- Visible **product/packshot** when relevant.
- **Strong first 2 seconds** for Reels.
- **Brand identity preserved** (see Brand Consistency Rule in content-system).
- **No AI-invented packaging** — real packshots only.
- **No spend without a hypothesis.**

## 4. Organic-to-Paid Workflow
1. **Organic tests the angle** (post/reel/story).
2. Measure against the account's own baseline (saves, shares, comments, message quality).
3. **Winning angle → ad candidate.** Promote only what proved demand organically.
4. Build the ad creative from the winning angle, keeping brand consistency.
5. Launch as a small test (with approval, and only if B2B unblocked when B2B).
6. Read results → pause / iterate / scale (see Decision Rules).

## 5. A/B Testing Rules
- Test **one variable at a time** (hook, format, audience, or offer).
- Define the **hypothesis before launch**.
- Give it **enough data** before judging.
- **Document learnings before scaling.**

## 8. Budget Allocation Rules
Allocate the total campaign budget by confidence level, not by gut feeling:

- **60–70%** → proven or highest-confidence angles.
- **20–30%** → controlled tests with a clear hypothesis.
- **10%** → experimental or exploratory creatives.
- **0%** → ideas that are not ready to spend yet.
- **No untested creative gets more than 30%** of the total budget.
- **Do not reallocate budget** until there is enough data to judge the test.
- If the **total budget is unknown**, recommend percentages only and ask Gonzalo for the total budget.
- If the **total budget is known**, show both the percentage and the ARS amount.

## 9. Decision Rules
- **Pause** if the metric is clearly below target after enough data.
- **Iterate** if hook/CTR is weak but engagement quality exists.
- **Scale** only if the metric **and** lead quality are acceptable.
- **Never judge only by CPC** — consider message quality and follow-up rate.

## 10. Canva / Automation Note
Canva can be considered later for template adaptation, brand kits, post/story variants, and production acceleration — but it must be **audited and approved before any integration**. Until then, facilitation = ready-to-paste copy + Canva Bulk Create sheets Aranza runs manually. No connector/MCP/API without explicit approval.

## 11. Safety Rules
- No campaign launch without Gonzalo's approval.
- No budget change without approval.
- No automatic publishing.
- No account access or connector use without explicit approval.
- B2B ads stay blocked until prices/margins/discount policy/follow-up owner are confirmed.
