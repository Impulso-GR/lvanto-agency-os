# AnimalFood — Extraordinary Content Standard

> The **minimum quality bar** for every AnimalFood content idea, calendar, reel, carousel, story, ad creative, caption, AI prompt, seller communication, or campaign proposal. If a recommendation does not clear this bar, it is **improved or rejected — never shipped generic.**
>
> Works together with: [[animalfood-trend-intelligence]] · [[animalfood-trend-signals-log]] · [[animalfood-column-map]] · [[animalfood-content-system]] · [[animalfood-creative-ads-lab]] · [[animalfood-paid-traffic-system]] · [[animalfood-operations-log]] · [[animalfood-daily-operator]] · `animalfood-daily-plan` skill.

---

## 1. Purpose

Prevent generic content and force **every** AnimalFood recommendation to be strategic, creative, measurable, brand-consistent, and delegable to Gonzalo or Aranza. This standard is the quality gate that sits on top of the content/ads/paid systems: nothing reaches Gonzalo as a proposal unless it is **extraordinary** by the definition below.

## 2. Definition of "extraordinary"

A recommendation is extraordinary only if it is **all** of:
- **not generic** and **not copied**;
- aligned to a **specific strategic pillar** (§5);
- aligned to the **specific brand's** tone and visual system (§6);
- useful for at least one of: **B2C demand · B2B trust · community · sales · retention · authority**;
- **measurable** (a real primary metric, §12);
- **easy to delegate** (clear owner + instructions, §11);
- **clear enough to produce** without guesswork;
- **strong enough to improve perceived value** of Gonzalo's work;
- **honest about evidence, confidence, and risk** (§14).

If it misses any of these, it is not extraordinary yet.

## 3. What is forbidden

- Generic calendars; posting for the sake of posting.
- Captions without strategy; prompts without brand style.
- Ideas without a metric; trends copied without adaptation.
- Content that does not feed a strategic pillar.
- Content that adds work without business value.
- Claims without evidence.
- Ads or budget suggestions without a hypothesis **and** a metric.

## 4. Required output for every content idea

Every idea must specify **all** of:
- **Brand**
- **Strategic pillar**
- **Business objective**
- **Audience**
- **Format**
- **Concept**
- **Hook**
- **Caption angle**
- **Visual direction**
- **AI image/video prompt** (if visual generation is needed)
- **Asset needed**
- **Owner:** Gonzalo / Aranza / Both
- **Difficulty**
- **Metric to validate**
- **Expected learning**
- **Evidence / Confidence**
- **Risk**
- **Next action**

A row missing any required field is **incomplete** and cannot be approved.

## 5. Strategic pillar system

Every piece must serve **exactly one primary** pillar (it may support others):
- **B2C demand**
- **B2B trust**
- **Product education**
- **Brand authority**
- **Community**
- **Commercial activation**
- **Distribution credibility**
- **Retention / loyalty**
- **Paid traffic testing**
- **Case study building for Lvanto**

If a piece cannot name its primary pillar, it fails §2 and is rejected.

## 6. Brand pillar mapping

- **Canfeed** — evidence-based premium education, performance, visible wellbeing, trust. Tone: *enseña*. Visual: #1E5A8E info / #B8732D CTA, cream uppercase humanist sans, golden-hour documentary, Tim Flach inspiration, **real packshots only**.
- **Catfeed** — feline-specific education, **not adapted dog content**, cat-nutrition specificity, owner awareness. Must read clearly **feline**.
- **IronPet** — accessibility, inclusion, practical nutrition, reliable everyday feeding (dog + cat lines).
- **Enercan / Enercat** — problem→solution, vitality, specific product benefit, active/functional positioning.
- **AnimalFood (mother account)** — institutional trust, distribution strength, family of brands, production/business credibility. Tone: *respalda*.
- **If a brand pillar is uncertain, ask Gonzalo instead of inventing.**

## 7. Creative quality rules

Every idea must include **at least one concrete creative improvement** — not just a topic:
- stronger hook;
- clearer hierarchy;
- better CTA;
- improved visual contrast;
- better use of the **real packshot**;
- stronger emotional angle;
- clearer commercial benefit;
- better WhatsApp/Instagram readability;
- better delegation to Aranza.

State which improvement the idea applies. "Nice idea" without an improvement does not qualify.

## 8. Caption quality rules

Captions must include: **hook · value · brand-consistent tone · clear CTA.** They must have **no exaggerated claims** and **no filler**. Match the brand tone verb (Canfeed *enseña*, etc.). Every claim must trace to a product fact (§18).

## 9. Visual prompt quality rules

AI image/video prompts must be **production-ready** and include:
- **subject**;
- **scene**;
- **lighting**;
- **camera / lens**;
- **composition**;
- **brand atmosphere**;
- **negative constraints** (what must NOT appear);
- **where the real packshot is composited** (never invent packaging — packshot is real and composited in).

## 10. Seller communication quality rules

Seller/B2B messages (WhatsApp, distributor comms) must:
- lead with the **commercial benefit** to the seller/distributor;
- be **factually accurate** (prices/margins confirmed, or marked **blocked** until confirmed);
- be **brand-consistent** and respectful of the *respalda* institutional tone;
- include a **clear ask / next step**;
- be **delegable** with exact text to send;
- never overpromise — claims backed by product facts only.

## 11. Delegation rules for Aranza

If a task is for Aranza, specify:
- **exact asset** to prepare;
- **template** to use (if known);
- **text** to place;
- **hierarchy** (what is primary/secondary);
- **what must not be changed** (brand colors, packshot, logo, tone);
- **what Gonzalo must review** before publishing;
- **expected delivery format** (story / reel / carousel / ad, dimensions).

## 12. Metrics and learning rules

Every piece must include at least one **primary metric**:
- saves · shares · comments · profile visits · DMs · link clicks · seller response · WhatsApp replies · reach quality · CTR · cost per result (if paid).

Every piece must also state an **expected learning** — what we will know after measuring it. Confirmed results flow to `05 · MÉTRICAS` and the operations log per [[animalfood-column-map]] (§12). Never invent metrics; never mark Measured without real data.

## 13. Design review rules

Before approval, evaluate (per the Creative Performance rule): **readability · hierarchy · text size · CTA clarity · visual focus · brand consistency · mobile-first impact · emotional hook · commercial purpose · contribution to community / demand / B2B perception.** Proactively recommend at least one improvement (bigger text, less noise, stronger hook, clearer CTA, more contrast, clearer packshot, more concrete benefit, format adaptation).

## 14. Trend intelligence requirement

Before proposing, **check [[animalfood-trend-signals-log]]** for a current signal (per the doctrine's Calendar/Paid gates).
- If a current signal supports the idea → cite it (source + confidence).
- **If current trend/performance data is missing → label the recommendation `HYPOTHESIS`** and state exactly **what signal is needed** to validate it (and add a *Needs data* row to the signals log).
- Internal performance (own data) outranks external trends on conflict.

## 15. Paid ads connection

A creative may be nominated as a **paid-traffic test** only when it meets the [[animalfood-paid-traffic-system]] + doctrine Paid Ads Gate: a **competitor/platform or internal-performance signal**, a stated **hypothesis**, a defined **metric** (e.g. CTR, cost per result), and a **risk** level. **B2B ads stay blocked** until prices/margins are confirmed. **No spend, audience, budget, or launch without Gonzalo's explicit approval.**

## 16. Decision rule: create / improve / reject

For every candidate, decide:
- **Create** — clears all of §2; produce it.
- **Improve** — strong core but missing a required field, a creative improvement, a metric, or brand fit → fix, then re-evaluate.
- **Reject** — generic, off-brand, no pillar, no metric, copied, or adds work without value.

Default to **Improve** over shipping a weak piece; default to **Reject** over forcing an off-brand trend.

## 18. Safety rules

- **No publishing without Gonzalo's approval.**
- **No ads or budget changes without Gonzalo's approval.**
- **No claims that are not backed by product facts** (nutrition, certifications, ingredients).
- **No unauthorized scraping**; no automated engagement (per doctrine §6/§21).
- **No generic content calendars.**
- Never invent packaging or metrics; real packshots and real data only.

## 19. Examples of weak vs strong recommendations

| | Weak (rejected) | Strong (extraordinary) |
|---|---|---|
| **Idea** | "Post about cat food." | "Catfeed carousel: *'Tu gato no es un perro chico'* — 3 feline-specific nutrition differences (taurina, proteína animal, hidratación)." |
| **Pillar** | none stated | Product education → B2C demand |
| **Hook** | "Check our cat food!" | "Tu gato no es un perro chico." |
| **Visual** | "nice cat photo" | Real Catfeed packshot, golden-hour, feline portrait (Tim Flach), #1E5A8E info pills; **not** a dog visual recolored. |
| **Metric** | none | Saves + comments vs cat-segment baseline. |
| **Evidence** | "cats are trending" (vague) | "Catfeed +6% YoY — **Low/Needs data**, label HYPOTHESIS until sourced (signals log)." |
| **Owner / delegation** | "someone posts it" | Gonzalo designs 5 slides → Aranza publishes + runs poll story (exact text specified). |

The difference is not effort — it's **strategy, brand specificity, measurability, honesty, and delegability.**
