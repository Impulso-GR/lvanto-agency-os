# AnimalFood — Growth System

## 1. Purpose

This file defines the **weekly growth operating system** for AnimalFood-related brands. It gives Gonzalo a repeatable loop to increase the value of his work, strengthen B2C community demand, improve B2B distributor perception, and build the brand group as a reference in Argentina — and later Chile, Paraguay, Uruguay, and Bolivia.

---

## 2. Strategic Objective

AnimalFood must build **visible consumer demand** so that B2B prospects (distributors, retailers, partners) see the brands as **profitable, active, trusted, and expanding**. B2C proof is the lever that unlocks B2B growth and higher value for Gonzalo's work.

---

## 3. Weekly Growth Loop

Run this loop every week:

1. **Trend research** — what is moving in pet food, pet care, and the target regions.
2. **Content opportunities** — angles worth posting this week.
3. **B2C community action** — one move to grow or activate the community.
4. **B2B distributor signal** — one visible proof point that signals demand/profitability to distributors.
5. **Campaign or promotion idea** — one promo, contest, or seasonal hook.
6. **Automation opportunity** — one task to automate (lead response, follow-up, content planning).
7. **Report / learning** — what worked, what didn't, key metric.
8. **Next action** — the single most important move for next week.

---

## 4. Brand Fronts

Keep each front distinct:

- **Animalfoodargentina** — mother account (group authority, umbrella perception).
- **Canfeed.ar** — dog focus.
- **Catfeed** — cat line inside Canfeed.
- **Ironpet.ar — dog lines.**
- **Ironpet.ar — cat lines.**
- **Enercan**
- **Enercat** — future launch.

---

## 5. Growth Priorities

- **Community building** — grow and activate B2C audience.
- **Distributor perception** — look profitable, active, and expanding.
- **Consumer trust** — reviews, results, transparency.
- **Visual authority** — premium, consistent brand image.
- **Product education** — teach value, ingredients, benefits.
- **Launch preparation** — groundwork for Enercat and future products.
- **Regional expansion perception** — signal readiness for CL, PY, UY, BO.
- **Lead capture systems** — turn attention into contacts and demand data.

---

## 6. Weekly Output

Every week the system should produce:

- **3 content angles**
- **1 campaign idea**
- **1 B2B signal idea**
- **1 automation idea**
- **1 measurable objective**
- **Roles for Gonzalo and Aranza/CM** (who does what this week)

---

## 7. Operating Cadence (decided)

- **Monday = Día de Comando** — Gonzalo decides and briefs the week.
- **Tuesday–Sunday** — execution (Aranza, once approved).
- **Friday 18h** — weekly measurement, one metric per account.
- **Anti-cruce:** one brand per post; no identical content across accounts.
- **B2C and B2B separated** at all times.

## 8. Account Priority Order (decided)

1. Cut content cross-over between accounts.
2. Accelerate IronPet.
3. Desaturate Enercan.
4. Structure Canfeed (keep momentum — it just "woke up").
5. Reconvert @animalfoodargentina to institutional/B2B.

## 9. Value Justification for Gonzalo

Build evidence that AnimalFood growth justifies a higher monthly value: systems, results, community, campaigns, automation, and strategic direction. Use proof like the 221-lead campaign (cuello = sales follow-up, not trafficking) to argue the system works. Targets and the income conflict live in `animalfood-context.md`.

## 10. Rules

- Separate AnimalFood from Lvanto and Sileoni.
- Prioritize practical execution over planning.
- Do not overload Aranza.
- Use **Gonzalo** for high-value creative direction, Photoshop, editing, and strategy.
- Use the **CM (Aranza)** for adaptation, publishing, and community routines.
- Focus on **business impact**, not just content volume.
- No publishing, outreach, automation, or paid traffic without human approval.
